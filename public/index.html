Urban Green Spaces Survey

Welcome!

This survey is about your experiences and opinions on the green areas in your neighborhood, specifically places like empty lots, overgrown fields, or green paths that aren't official parks but are still full of life. These spaces, often overlooked or waiting to be developed, are what we call "informal green spaces." They're not planned or designed by the city, but many of us use them for walking, relaxing, or just enjoying some quiet nature. Birds, squirrels, insects, boars, and even beavers might call these places home too.

We want to understand:
• How do you use these places
• What do you see and feel when you're there
• How do people and wildlife share the space
• What do you hope for the future of these places

Your answers will help us learn how to design cities better so that they can become home to both people and nature. You'll also get a chance to tell us about your favorite spots within the green space, such as tell us about animals you've seen, and share your ideas for what these places could become.

Your perspective is important to us as the results of the study will be communicated to the urban authorities. By continuing, you agree your answers will be collected anonymously for research purposes.

Thank you for taking the time to share your thoughts.

Thank you for participating in this survey. Your responses from the Likert-scale ratings and wildlife observations to the map annotations and written answers will collectively inform a Multispecies Urban Justice framework. In analysis, we will look for patterns where human values and ecological values overlap (indicating opportunities for synergy) and where they diverge (indicating conflicts to resolve). This will help urban planners and researchers propose solutions that benefit both local communities and the non-human species sharing our city.

Your input is a crucial step toward more inclusive, equitable green space planning for all urban inhabitants, human and otherwise. :-)
